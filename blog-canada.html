Study in Canada: Complete Guide for International Students in 2025

Huda Imran
January 4, 2025
8 min read
Education
Home Blog Study in Canada 2025

Canada is one of the most popular destinations for international students — and it's easy to see why. Known for its world-class education system, multicultural environment, and outstanding post-graduation opportunities, Canada offers a unique combination of academic excellence and quality of life that few countries can match.

In this complete guide from NexGen Dubai, we'll walk you through everything you need to know: the benefits of studying in Canada, top universities, the visa application process, estimated living costs, scholarships, and the best cities to build your future.

1. Why Study in Canada?

Canada consistently ranks among the world's top study destinations for international students. Here's what makes it stand out:

  • World-Class Education: Home to some of the best universities globally, offering a wide range of undergraduate, graduate, and postgraduate programs across every discipline.
  • Cultural Diversity: Canada celebrates multiculturalism. Students interact with peers from over 190 nationalities, creating a uniquely rich and inclusive learning environment.
  • Safety and Quality of Life: Consistently ranked as one of the safest countries in the world, with excellent healthcare, public services, and a high standard of living.
  • Post-Graduation Opportunities: International graduates can stay in Canada to gain work experience and apply for permanent residency through programs like the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP).
  • Affordable Compared to the US and UK: Tuition fees and living costs are generally more competitive, making Canada excellent value for the quality of education received.

2. Top Universities in Canada for International Students

Canada boasts a range of world-renowned institutions. Here are the top universities to consider for your studies:

University of Toronto

Consistently ranked among the top 30 universities globally. Known for its cutting-edge research and diverse programs, the University of Toronto attracts students from all over the world across sciences, engineering, medicine, law, and the arts.

McGill University

Located in Montreal, McGill is one of Canada's oldest and most prestigious universities — renowned for its exceptional medical, science, and liberal arts programs. It consistently ranks among the top universities in North America.

University of British Columbia (UBC)

Based in Vancouver, UBC offers a vast range of undergraduate and graduate programs in engineering, business, science, and the arts. Known for its research-driven approach, beautiful campus, and strong industry connections.

McMaster University

Located in Hamilton, Ontario, McMaster is recognised for its high-quality medical and engineering programs. Its collaborative teaching style and strong research output attract students from around the world.

University of Montreal

Based in Quebec, the University of Montreal offers programs in both French and English, making it ideal for bilingual students. One of Canada's leading research institutions with a vibrant student city around it.

Known for its commitment to student success, Algoma offers undergraduate programs in arts, science, and business with a strong emphasis on personalised learning and small class sizes. 3. How to Apply for a Student Visa (Study Permit)

To study in Canada, international students need to apply for a study permit. Here is a step-by-step guide to the process:

  1. Check Eligibility: Ensure you have acceptance from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI), proof of sufficient funds, no criminal record, and a clean medical history. A medical exam may be required.
  2. Prepare Your Documents: Key documents include your letter of acceptance from a Canadian university, proof of identity (passport and photos), and proof of financial support to cover tuition, living expenses, and return travel.
  3. Apply Online: Submit your study permit application through the official Government of Canada website. Pay the processing fee (CAD $150) and await a decision — typically 4–12 weeks.
  4. Biometrics and Interview: Depending on your country of origin, you may need to provide biometrics (fingerprints and photos) at a Visa Application Centre, or attend an interview.
  5. Receive Your Study Permit: If approved, you'll receive a letter of introduction. Your physical study permit will be issued at the port of entry when you arrive in Canada.

4. Living Costs in Canada

Canada offers relatively affordable living costs compared to popular destinations like the USA or the UK. However, costs vary depending on the city and lifestyle. Here's a general breakdown:

Expense CategoryEstimated Cost (Per Year)
Tuition Fees (International)CAD $7,000 – $29,000
Accommodation (per month)CAD $400 – $1,500
Food & Groceries (per month)CAD $300 – $600
Transportation (per month)CAD $100 – $200
Health Insurance (annually)CAD $600 – $1,000
Books & Supplies (annually)CAD $500 – $1,500

Overall, a student can expect to spend between CAD $18,000 and $35,000 per year on tuition and living expenses, depending on the university and city chosen.

5. Scholarships for International Students

Funding your Canadian education is more achievable than many students realise. Here are some key scholarship opportunities:

  • Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships: CAD $50,000/year for doctoral students demonstrating academic excellence and leadership.
  • Canada Graduate Scholarships (NSERC / SSHRC / CIHR): Available for masters and doctoral students in natural sciences, social sciences, and health research.
  • University-Specific Scholarships: Most Canadian universities offer entrance scholarships, merit awards, and bursaries for international students. Check each university's international student office for details.
  • Algoma University Awards: As a NexGen partner institution, Algoma offers various awards specifically designed to support international students. Ask our counselors for details.
  • Province-Based Funding: Several Canadian provinces offer grants and loans for international students that can supplement university funding.

6. Best Cities to Study in Canada

  • Toronto: Canada's largest city — a global hub for finance, technology, and the arts. Home to the University of Toronto and York University. Vibrant multicultural population with endless career opportunities.
  • Vancouver: Known for its natural beauty, mild climate, and world-class universities. UBC and SFU are major draws. Strong tech sector and growing film industry.
  • Montreal: A bilingual city with a vibrant student life and more affordable living costs than Toronto or Vancouver. Home to McGill and Concordia universities.
  • Ottawa: The nation's capital — home to the University of Ottawa and Carleton University. Government jobs are abundant, and the city offers a high standard of living with a lower cost than Toronto.
  • Sault Ste. Marie (Algoma): A smaller, more focused academic environment with a close-knit community. Algoma University offers a highly personalised educational experience with lower tuition and living costs — ideal for students who want quality education without the big-city price tag.
